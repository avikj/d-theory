## XI. SPECIFIC FACTUAL ERRORS/OMISSIONS

### Error 1: "No Overlap with Historical Vedic Mathematics"

**Wikipedia claims**: Tirtha's book has "practically nothing in common" with Vedic-period math

**True for**: Tirtha's specific claims about his book being ancient

**But misleading because**:
- Some techniques DO resemble ancient methods
- Multiplication algorithms similar to Vedic śulbasūtras
- Article doesn't distinguish what's actually ancient vs. Tirtha's inventions

### Error 2: Decimals Unknown in Vedic Period

**Wikipedia states**: "Decimals...were unknown during the Vedic times and were introduced in India only in the sixteenth century"

**Actually**:
- Decimal **place-value system** was Indian invention (~500 CE)
- Brahmagupta (628 CE) used decimals
- Al-Khwarizmi brought Indian decimals to Arab world (~825 CE)
- Europe got "Arabic numerals" from India via Arabs

**Wikipedia confuses**: Decimal **fractions** vs. Decimal **system**

### Error 3: No Calculus in Vedic Period

**Wikipedia states**: "Vedic India had no knowledge of differentiation or integration"

**True for**: Vedic period specifically (1500-500 BCE)

**But misleading because**:
- Kerala school (1350-1500 CE) developed calculus precursors
- 200+ years before Newton/Leibniz
- Wikipedia doesn't mention this in "Vedic Mathematics" article
- Creates impression India never had calculus
