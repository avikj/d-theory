The `left-identity` law was proven as follows:
```agda
D-is-Monad .Monad.left-identity x f =
  begin
    (ι x >>= f)
  ≡⟨ refl ⟩
    mu (D-map f (ι x))
  ≡⟨ refl ⟩
    mu (D-map f (x , x , refl))
  ≡⟨ refl ⟩
    mu ((f x) , (f x) , cong f refl)
  ≡⟨ refl ⟩
    let (x_f , y_f , p_f) = f x in
    let (x_f' , y_f' , p_f') = f x in -- This line is redundant but harmless
    let q = cong f refl in
    (x_f , y_f , p_f ∙ (q.snd .fst))
  ≡⟨ cong (λ q_y → x_f , y_f , p_f ∙ q_y) (cong-path-snd-fst-refl f x) ⟩
    (x_f , y_f , p_f ∙ refl)
  ≡⟨ cong (λ p' → x_f , y_f , p') (Path.∙-idR p_f) ⟩
    (x_f , y_f , p_f)
  ∎
  where
    -- Helper lemma: (cong f refl) .snd .fst is refl
    cong-path-snd-fst-refl : ∀ {X Y : Type} (f : X → D Y) (x : X) → (cong f refl {x = x} .snd .fst) ≡ refl
    cong-path-snd-fst-refl f x i = refl
```

**Explanation of the Proof:**

1.  **`ι x >>= f`:** Starts with the left side of the identity.
2.  **`mu (D-map f (ι x))`:** Expands `_>>=_` definition.
3.  **`mu (D-map f (x , x , refl))`:** Expands `ι x`.
4.  **`mu ((f x) , (f x) , cong f refl)`:** Expands `D-map f (x, x, refl)`. Here, `f x` is an element of `D Y`, so `(f x)` is `(x_f, y_f, p_f)`. `cong f refl` is a path between `(f x)` and `(f x)`.
5.  **`let (x_f , y_f , p_f) = f x in ... (x_f , y_f , p_f ∙ (q.snd .fst))`:** This step applies the definition of `mu`.
    *   `x_1` from `mu` is `x_f`.
    *   `y_2` from `mu` is `y_f`.
    *   `p_1` from `mu` is `p_f`.
    *   `q` from `mu` is `cong f refl`.
    *   `(q.snd .fst)` is `(cong f refl .snd .fst)`. This is the path between the `y` components of `(f x)` and `(f x)`, which is `y_f ≡ y_f`, i.e., `refl`.
6.  **`cong (λ q_y → x_f , y_f , p_f ∙ q_y) (cong-path-snd-fst-refl f x)`:** This step uses the helper lemma `cong-path-snd-fst-refl` to show that `(cong f refl .snd .fst)` is indeed `refl`.
7.  **`(x_f , y_f , p_f ∙ refl)`:** Simplifies the path component.
8.  **`cong (λ p' → x_f , y_f , p') (Path.∙-idR p_f)`:** Uses the `Path.∙-idR` lemma (right identity for path composition) to simplify `p_f ∙ refl` to `p_f`.
9.  **`(x_f , y_f , p_f)`:** This is exactly `f x`, thus completing the proof.

**Helper Lemma:**
The crucial helper lemma `cong-path-snd-fst-refl` was defined to show that `(cong f refl {x = x} .snd .fst)` is `refl`. This lemma is proven by path induction (`i = refl`).
