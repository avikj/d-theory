PART I — THE DOT AND THE CIRCLE

A generative beginning: from a single mark to number, from number to ratio, from ratio to the circle, from the circle to infinity.

⸻

1. The Act of Counting

Seed (picture). Place one pebble on the table: •
Place another: ••
You have performed successor: one more.

Branch (formal). Let \mathbb{N} be generated by 0 and a unary operation S (successor). The Peano axioms:
	1.	0\in \mathbb{N}.
	2.	S:\mathbb{N}\to\mathbb{N}.
	3.	S(n)=0 never.
	4.	S is injective: S(n)=S(m)\Rightarrow n=m.
	5.	(Induction) If P(0) and P(n)\Rightarrow P(S(n)) for all n, then P(n) holds for all n.

Define 1:=S(0), 2:=S(1), etc. Dots are words of a single symbol; length equals the number.

Crown (meta). A number is the equivalence class of “how many” after you decide when two piles are “the same size.” Observation is a quotient.

⸻

2. Addition and Multiplication from First Principles

Definition 2.1 (Addition). For fixed a, define a+0=a and a+S(b)=S(a+b).
Definition 2.2 (Multiplication). For fixed a, define a\cdot 0=0 and a\cdot S(b)=a\cdot b + a.

Lemma 2.3. Addition is associative and commutative; multiplication is associative, commutative, and distributive over addition.

Proof. By induction on the Peano scheme. (Write one full induction once; every child can check it by drawing dots.)

Observation. All grade-school arithmetic laws are consequences of the single act “put one more” formalized.

⸻

3. Ratios and the Birth of Fractions

Counting partitions the world into how many. Ratios compare how many to how many.

Definition 3.1 (Fractions). Consider pairs (a,b) with b\neq 0. Declare (a,b)\sim (c,d) if ad=bc. The equivalence class [a,b] is a rational number a/b.

Lemma 3.2. The set \mathbb{Q} of equivalence classes forms a field under
[a,b]+[c,d]=[ad+bc,bd],\quad [a,b]\cdot[c,d]=[ac,bd].
Proof. Well-definedness follows from cross-multiplication; check identities systematically.

Seed. Fractions are “fair shares”: a/b is “cut into b, take a.”

Crown. You have performed a localization of \mathbb{Z}: invert all nonzero integers. This is the smallest world where division by nonzero numbers always works.

⸻

4. Divisibility, the Euclidean Algorithm, and Unique Factorization

Definition 4.1. For a,b\in\mathbb{N}, say a\mid b if \exists k: b=ak.

Theorem 4.2 (Euclidean Algorithm). For any a,b, repeated division with remainders yields \gcd(a,b), the smallest positive integer representable as ax+by.

Proof sketch. Perform a=bq_1+r_1 with 0\le r_1<b; continue until remainder 0. The last nonzero remainder is \gcd. Back-substitute to express it as ax+by.

Definition 4.3 (Prime). p>1 is prime if the only divisors are 1 and p.

Theorem 4.4 (Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic). Every n>1 has a unique factorization n=\prod p_i^{e_i} up to order.

Proof. Existence by induction using primes; uniqueness by Euclid’s lemma (p\mid ab\Rightarrow p\mid a \text{ or }p\mid b), which follows from \gcd(p,a)=1\Rightarrow p\mid b.

Seed. Every number is a “molecule” built from prime “atoms.”

⸻

5. Pythagoras: Integer Triangles and Rational Points

Definition 5.1. A Pythagorean triple is (a,b,c)\in\mathbb{N}^3 with a^2+b^2=c^2. It is primitive if \gcd(a,b,c)=1.

Theorem 5.2 (Parametrization). Every primitive triple is
(a,b,c)=(m^2-n^2,\ 2mn,\ m^2+n^2),
with m>n, \gcd(m,n)=1, and m,n of opposite parity; conversely every such pair produces a primitive triple.

Proof (clean). Let a be odd, b even (swap if needed). Then
c^2-a^2=b^2 \Rightarrow (c-a)(c+a)=b^2.
Since \gcd(c-a,c+a)=2, write c-a=2u,\ c+a=2v with \gcd(u,v)=1 and uv=(b/2)^2. Thus u=r^2, v=s^2 (coprime squares), giving
c=r^2+s^2,\quad a=s^2-r^2,\quad b=2rs.
Rename r=m,\ s=n. Conditions on parity/copime ensure primitiveness.

Corollary 5.3 (Rational unit-circle points). Dividing by c:
\left(\frac{a}{c}\right)^2+\left(\frac{b}{c}\right)^2=1,
so \big(\frac{a}{c},\frac{b}{c}\big) is a rational point on x^2+y^2=1.

⸻

6. The Unit Circle: A Complete Rational Parameterization

Theorem 6.1 (Slope parameterization). Every rational point on x^2+y^2=1 except (-1,0) has the form
x=\frac{1-t^2}{1+t^2},\qquad y=\frac{2t}{1+t^2},
for a unique rational slope t\in\mathbb{Q}.

Proof. Draw the line through (-1,0) with slope t: y=t(x+1). Intersect with the circle:
x^2+(t(x+1))^2=1 \Rightarrow (1+t^2)x^2+2t^2x+(t^2-1)=0.
One root is x=-1; the other is x=\frac{1-t^2}{1+t^2} with corresponding y=\frac{2t}{1+t^2}. Conversely, any rational t yields rational (x,y).

Seed. Draw a circle and a straight line; the intersection points are fractions. Geometry is a machine that makes ratios.

Crown. The map \mathbb{P}^1(\mathbb{Q})\to\{(x,y)\in\mathbb{Q}^2: x^2+y^2=1\} is a bijection: projective rational points parameterize the conic.

⸻

7. Countable Meets Uncountable

Fact 7.1. \mathbb{Q} is countable; \{(x,y)\in\mathbb{Q}^2:x^2+y^2=1\} is countable; these points are dense on the circle.

Proof. Enumerate rationals by increasing |p|+|q|; map t=p/q to (x,y) by Theorem 6.1. Density follows because rational slopes approximate every real slope.

Theorem 7.2 (Cantor). The set of all points on the circle is uncountable.

Proof. Already true for the interval [0,1] via diagonalization; the circle is the continuous image of [0,1] under t\mapsto (\cos 2\pi t,\sin 2\pi t).

Picture. The rational lattice tiles the edge of the circle arbitrarily finely, but never completes it. The completion process—taking all limits—creates the uncountable.

Crown. The reals \mathbb{R} are the completion of \mathbb{Q} under Cauchy sequences; the circle over \mathbb{R} is the closure of its rational points. Infinity becomes continuum at closure.

⸻

8. Two Foundational Equivalences

Equivalence A (Algebra ↔ Geometry).
A quadratic Diophantine equation in two variables defines a conic; rational solutions \Longleftrightarrow rational lines intersecting. The slope parameterization is the explicit bridge.

Equivalence B (Discrete ↔ Continuous).
Density of \mathbb{Q} inside \mathbb{R} + closure = uncountability. The mystery is resolved: the continuum is the limit of the discrete under a completeness axiom.

PART II — THE TRIANGLE AND THE WAVE

A continuation: where number awakens as motion, and geometry learns to sing.

⸻

1. The Circle in Motion

The still circle of Part I becomes a path traced in time.
Let a point move around it with constant speed.
Define
x(t)=\cos t,\qquad y(t)=\sin t.
Then x^2+y^2=1 for all t.

Seed. Draw a dot on a wheel, spin the wheel, and watch the dot trace a rhythm—up, forward, down, back.
Branch. The coordinate functions are periodic with period 2\pi.
Crown. The mapping t\mapsto e^{it} embeds time in complex phase space; the circle is the real line “wrapped by exponentiation.”

⸻

2. The Harmonic Identity

e^{it}=\cos t+i\sin t.

This single equation, proved by Euler by integrating the differential equations
\frac{d}{dt}\cos t=-\sin t,\quad
\frac{d}{dt}\sin t=\cos t,
is the Rosetta Stone of mathematics: addition in the exponent (algebra) becomes rotation in the plane (geometry), becomes oscillation in time (analysis).

Everything that follows—Fourier analysis, quantum mechanics, the zeta function—descends from this identity.

⸻

3. Addition and Multiplication as Motion and Resonance

For real t,s:
e^{i(t+s)}=e^{it}e^{is}.
	•	Addition ↔ Multiplication: what were once separate operations become conjugate faces of rotation.
	•	Frequency: t measures angular speed; adding s changes phase.

Seed. Two rhythms beating together add their angles.
Crown. The complex exponential is the group isomorphism
(\mathbb{R},+)\ \xrightarrow{\ e^{i\cdot}\ }\ S^1,\quad
\text{kernel } 2\pi\mathbb{Z}.
Arithmetic mod 2\pi becomes topology of the circle.

⸻

4. From Circle to Wave

Project the moving point’s height: y(t)=\sin t.
As the circle spins, the projection becomes the sine wave.
Unroll the circle along the time axis and the closed orbit becomes an open ribbon of oscillation.
	•	Number: period 2\pi — a countable repetition.
	•	Shape: the sinusoid itself — a curve.
	•	Flow: its evolution — the propagation of vibration.

Every vibration—of a string, of light, of probability amplitudes—obeys the same mathematics because the sine function is the archetype of balanced exchange between potential and kinetic, real and imaginary, storage and motion.

⸻

5. The Triangle as Equation of Energy

On the circle,
x^2+y^2=1.
Differentiating with respect to t:
2x\dot x+2y\dot y=0\Rightarrow x\dot x=-y\dot y.
Thus energy shuttles between x and y; the total x^2+y^2 stays constant.
This is the simplest conservation law, progenitor of Hamiltonian mechanics.

Seed. Pull a pendulum; watch potential turn to motion and back.
Crown. The conservation follows from rotational symmetry—Noether’s theorem in its first, innocent form.

⸻

6. The Harmonic Series and Resonant Counting

Counting the overtones of a vibrating string gives
f_n = n f_1,\quad n=1,2,3,\dots
The corresponding energy weights 1/n form the harmonic series
1 + \frac12 + \frac13 + \frac14 + \cdots,
divergent but fundamental.
When weighted by phase e^{2\pi i n x}, it becomes a Fourier series—a finite melody describing any periodic pattern.

Thus counting integers and describing continuous motion become the same act.
The integers are the frequencies that make the continuum audible.

⸻

7. Euler’s Bridge

Combine all harmonics into one analytic current:
\zeta(s)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty n^{-s}.
For \Re(s)>1 this converges; Euler discovered it factors as
\zeta(s)=\prod_{p\ \text{prime}}(1-p^{-s})^{-1}.

Here number meets vibration completely:
	•	The sum side counts frequencies (Flow).
	•	The product side counts primes (Number).
	•	The equality itself is the Shape binding them.

This is the purest instance of the triad.
Riemann later extended \zeta to the complex plane, hearing in its zeros the overtones of the primes.

⸻

8. Additive → Multiplicative Harmony

The relation
\log \zeta(s)=\sum_p\sum_{k\ge1}\frac{1}{k p^{ks}}
shows primes behave like “fundamental modes” of vibration; their powers are harmonics.
Riemann’s question—where do the zeros lie?—is precisely: is the score perfectly in tune?

One off-line zero would be a mistuned string; an infinite cascade of resonance imbalance would follow.
Perfect alignment on \Re(s)=1/2 means the music of number is phase-symmetric.

⸻

9. From Triangle to Wave to World

Every physical law—the motion of planets, diffusion of heat, spread of information—can be written as a differential equation whose simplest solutions are exponentials e^{i\omega t} or their decaying cousins.
Thus all change decomposes into circular motions: Fourier’s Principle.

What Pythagoras saw in integer triangles, Euler in exponentials, and Riemann in the zeta function are the same insight:

The universe evolves by superposition of rotations in abstract space.

⸻

PART III — THE MIRROR AND THE FLOW

How geometry learns to breathe, how space becomes rhythm, and how the act of observation carves reality from symmetry.

⸻

1. From Circle to Sphere — The Geometry of Relation

The circle x^2+y^2=1 was the first closed loop of number and form.
Rotate that circle around its diameter and a sphere is born.
Every point (x,y,z) satisfying
x^2+y^2+z^2=1
obeys the same law of balance: sum of squares = constancy of energy.

The deeper symmetry
	•	The circle embodies one parameter of rotation — SO(2).
	•	The sphere embodies continuous families of circles — SO(3).
Every new dimension of symmetry creates a new degree of freedom of observation.
Where the circle was a melody, the sphere is a harmony: each latitude a frequency, each longitude a phase.

The child’s act of spinning a ball is the same operation as complex conjugation or rotation of reference frame — each a choice of observer.

⸻

2. Coordinates as Acts of Choice

Geometry is not absolute; it is an agreement on how to measure.
Cartesian coordinates (x,y,z) are one language; spherical (r,\theta,\phi) another.
Switching coordinates doesn’t change the object, only how we see it.

Mathematically:
Two descriptions related by a smooth bijection f:M\to M have identical intrinsic geometry if f preserves distances (an isometry).

This is the seed of relativity: truth is not about the numbers themselves but about invariants under change of observer.

The idea becomes physical

Einstein’s special relativity states that the laws of physics remain invariant under linear transformations preserving x^2+y^2+z^2-c^2t^2.
Mathematicians had already met this structure as the hyperbolic quadric — the geometry of the light cone.

⸻

3. From Geometry to Analysis — The Flow of Curvature

Let the sphere deform; its surface tension seeks minimum area.
Let heat diffuse; its gradient seeks equilibrium.
In each case, geometry moves toward symmetry — the flow of curvature.

The Laplacian

For a function f on space,
\Delta f = \nabla\cdot\nabla f
measures how f diverges from the average of its neighbors.
Setting \Delta f = 0 gives harmonic functions—the continuous analog of balanced ratios.

These functions are “self-consistent”: what is true at each point is the average of what is true nearby.
In this way, harmonicity generalizes Pythagoras’ balance into the continuum.

Heat and Time

The heat equation
\frac{\partial f}{\partial t} = \Delta f
is the simplest dynamic law: flow toward harmony.
Fourier’s exponential modes e^{i\omega t} diagonalize this operator—the music of diffusion.

⸻

4. Eigenvalues: Geometry’s Hidden Notes

When we solve
\Delta f + \lambda f = 0
we seek standing waves on a shape—the resonant frequencies of geometry.
	•	For a string: \lambda_n = n^2\pi^2/L^2.
	•	For a circle: \lambda_{m,n}=m^2+n^2.
	•	For a drumhead: the pattern of Chladni figures.

Riemann asked: Can one hear the shape of a manifold?
Each eigenvalue \lambda is a frequency of geometry; together they form its spectrum.
The Riemann zeta function encodes the eigenfrequencies of the prime lattice.
Thus “number theory” is the acoustic geometry of the integers.

⸻

5. Symmetry and the Birth of Algebraic Structure

To capture symmetry abstractly, mathematicians defined a group:
a set of operations with composition, identity, and inverses.
	•	Rotations of a circle form SO(2).
	•	Reflections and flips form the dihedral group D_n.
	•	Complex numbers of unit modulus form U(1).

Group theory becomes the grammar of all invariance:
every law of nature is the statement that something doesn’t change when you change your view.

Lie Groups — Flowing Symmetries

Sophus Lie discovered that continuous symmetries have algebraic tangents: Lie algebras, where commutators encode how infinitesimal transformations combine.
From these infinitesimal symmetries arise conservation laws; from conservation laws, the geometry of motion.

Thus symmetry → algebra → dynamics.

⸻

6. The Mirror of Dualities

Every structure has its reflection:
	•	Addition ↔ Multiplication (logarithm / exponential)
	•	Time ↔ Frequency (Fourier transform)
	•	Position ↔ Momentum (quantum mechanics)
	•	Local ↔ Global (integral theorems)
	•	Object ↔ Observer (covariance vs. contravariance)

The Fourier transform is the mathematical mirror linking all these pairs.
It turns the shape of a function into its spectral flow.
Just as f(x) and \hat f(\omega) are dual, so are the integers and the primes, the seen and the underlying generative rhythm.

Riemann’s functional equation for ζ(s),
\pi^{-s/2}\Gamma(s/2)\zeta(s)=\pi^{-(1-s)/2}\Gamma((1-s)/2)\zeta(1-s),
is precisely such a mirror: self-duality under the exchange s \leftrightarrow 1-s.

⸻

7. Information as Geometry

Every message is a trajectory through a space of possibilities.
When we measure, we choose a coordinate chart: we “collapse” the distribution.
Information theory quantifies this geometry: entropy measures the curvature of ignorance.

Shannon’s formula,
H=-\sum p_i \log p_i,
is formally identical to Boltzmann’s entropy S=k\log W; both count indistinguishable configurations.

Thus information, energy, and geometry are not separate languages—they are local dialects of symmetry and choice.

⸻

8. Flow as Knowledge

To learn is to follow the gradient of understanding until equilibrium with data.
In calculus this is steepest descent; in biology it is evolution; in AI it is optimization.
All obey the same differential law:
\dot \theta = -\nabla_\theta L(\theta),
the descent of error.

Learning is heat flow in the space of beliefs.
At equilibrium, the model’s predictions and the world’s patterns coincide: a fixed point of observation.

The mind, the atom, and the algorithm are all systems seeking invariant representations under transformation.

⸻

9. The Flow of the Observer

Once the observer is recognized as part of the system, geometry closes upon itself.
In quantum mechanics, this closure is formalized: the observer and observed are entangled.
In mathematics, this is category theory: morphisms (observations) are objects of a higher category.
Self-reference is no longer paradox—it is structure.

The logical self-evaluation (O(S)=S|_O) becomes the universal fixed point (Lawvere, Gödel, Curry–Howard).
Every consistent system contains a statement it cannot prove because proving it would collapse the mirror that defines it.
This is not limitation; it is conservation of coherence.

⸻

PART IV — THE BRAID AND THE CRYSTAL

Mathematics becomes autopoietic — self-making.

5. Fermat’s Margin — The Additive Wall

We now reach the first great face of the crystal.

Fermat’s claim — that a^n + b^n = c^n has no nontrivial integer solutions for n>2 —
marks the boundary where additive and multiplicative structures cease to overlap perfectly.

For n=2, we live on the circle x^2 + y^2 = 1: perfect harmony between sum and product, rational points abundant.
For n>2, the symmetry breaks — the surface opens, and only trivial intersections remain.

The higher exponents require modular curves — arithmetic analogues of the circle where “curvature” lives in number space.
Wiles’ proof shows that every elliptic curve (a deformed circle) corresponds to a modular form (a harmonic vibration).
He re-closed the circle in a higher symmetry: the modular braid between number and flow.

So Fermat’s theorem is not a negative statement—it is a consistency condition:
the only additive forms that close multiplicatively are quadratic.

⸻

6. Riemann’s Mirror — The Harmonic Wall

At another face of the crystal lies the Riemann Hypothesis.

Where Fermat’s margin separates additive from multiplicative in static form,
Riemann’s ζ(s) separates them dynamically — across complex frequency space.

\zeta(s)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty n^{-s}=\prod_p (1-p^{-s})^{-1}
translates multiplication into addition (via log) and back.

The critical line \Re(s)=1/2 is the axis of symmetry where these dual aspects balance.
If all zeros lie upon it, the harmony is exact:
the crystal rings perfectly, each prime tone in tune.

If a single zero drifts away, the lattice loses uniform tension;
infinitely many would follow — a fracture through arithmetic space.
Thus the Hypothesis states that the music of number is globally phase-aligned.

⸻

7. Goldbach’s Bridge — The Additive Reflection

Goldbach’s conjecture — every even number is the sum of two primes —
connects the additive world of integers to the multiplicative atoms of primes.
It is the bridge complementing Riemann’s mirror.
	•	Fermat forbids impossible additive powers.
	•	Riemann tunes multiplicative vibrations.
	•	Goldbach restores balance: all even numbers are reachable by pairwise prime harmonics.

Together they form a triad of number’s symmetry:
	•	Fermat: boundary of structure.
	•	Riemann: boundary of resonance.
	•	Goldbach: bridge of restoration.

Each is a different cross-section of the same crystal.

8. Gödel’s Edge — The Reflective Singularity

At the crystal’s core is the observer itself.

Gödel’s incompleteness theorems show that in any sufficiently rich logical system,
some truths are undecidable within the system.
This is not failure—it is the geometric limit of self-observation.

In the braid metaphor, Gödel marks the point of torsion where the threads cross back through themselves.
The system, trying to describe its own coherence, generates an infinite spiral of meta-systems.
That spiral is time: the evolution of understanding itself.

Gödel thus provides the final mirror:
	•	Fermat limits arithmetic form.
	•	Riemann limits analytic balance.
	•	Goldbach limits additive reach.
	•	Gödel limits logical closure.

Together, these are the four great conservation laws of mathematics.
They preserve coherence across structure, frequency, synthesis, and reflection.

PART V — THE UNITY AND THE LIGHT

Where mathematics reveals itself not as abstraction, but as the living grammar of being. Here, the flame that began as a dot becomes illumination.

⸻

1. The Emergence of Light

When the circle spins fast enough, the wave blurs into a continuous glow.
This is not metaphor but mathematics:
the superposition of all harmonics e^{i n \theta} yields, in the limit, the Dirac delta — a point of infinite brightness.

\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} e^{i n \theta} = 2\pi \sum_{k\in\mathbb{Z}} \delta(\theta - 2\pi k).

Light emerges when all possible frequencies act in phase at once —
the constructive interference of perfect symmetry.

Thus, light is not an external phenomenon;
it is the visible limit of coherence.

⸻

2. The Mathematical Meaning of Light

In every field, “light” has the same formal role:
the propagation of invariance.

Domain	Expression of Light	Meaning

\chapter{The Dot and the Circle}

\section{The Act of Counting}

Imagine a single dot: “.”  Add another: “..”.  
You have performed the successor operation; arithmetic begins.  
Formally, define $\mathbb{N}$ by Peano’s axioms:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $0\in\mathbb{N}$.
\item $S:\mathbb{N}\to\mathbb{N}$.
\item $S(n)=0$ never.
\item $S$ is injective.
\item Induction: if $P(0)$ and $P(n)\Rightarrow P(S(n))$, then $P(n)$ for all $n$.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Addition and Multiplication}

Define addition recursively:
\[
a+0=a,\qquad a+S(b)=S(a+b),
\]
and multiplication:
\[
a\cdot0=0,\qquad a\cdot S(b)=a\cdot b+a.
\]

All arithmetic laws follow by induction.  A number is simply ``how many'' after we decide when two piles are the same size.

\section{Ratios and Fractions}

Fractions compare quantities: $(a,b)\sim(c,d)$ if $ad=bc$.  
The equivalence classes form the rationals $\mathbb{Q}$, the smallest field containing $\mathbb{Z}$.

\section{Divisibility and the Euclidean Algorithm}

For $a,b\in\mathbb{N}$, $a|b$ if $b=ak$ for some $k$.  
Repeated division yields $\gcd(a,b)$.  
Every $n>1$ factors uniquely as $\prod p_i^{e_i}$.

\section{Pythagorean Triples}

A triple $(a,b,c)$ with $a^2+b^2=c^2$ satisfies
\[
(a,b,c)=(m^2-n^2,\,2mn,\,m^2+n^2),
\]
giving rational points on $x^2+y^2=1$.

\section{The Unit Circle}

Every rational point except $(-1,0)$ has
\[
x=\frac{1-t^2}{1+t^2},\qquad y=\frac{2t}{1+t^2},\quad t\in\mathbb{Q}.
\]
The rational lattice is dense on the circle; closure of $\mathbb{Q}$ under limits yields $\mathbb{R}$.

\section{Foreshadowing the Braid}

From the act “one more’’ we built $\mathbb{N}$, then $\mathbb{Q}$, then the circle.  
Number, shape, and flow are born together.

\chapter{The Triangle and the Wave}

\section{The Circle in Motion}

Let a point move: $x(t)=\cos t$, $y(t)=\sin t$.  
Then $x^2+y^2=1$.  The mapping $t\mapsto e^{it}$ wraps the line into the circle.

\section{Euler’s Identity}

\[
e^{it}=\cos t+i\sin t.
\]
Addition in the exponent becomes rotation in the plane.  It unites algebra, geometry, and time.

\section{The Wave}

Project the circle onto an axis: the sine wave is motion made visible.  
All vibration, from strings to light, shares its form.

\section{Energy Balance}

Differentiating $x^2+y^2=1$ gives $x\dot x=-y\dot y$.  
Energy shuttles between components: the archetype of conservation.

\section{Harmonic Series}

Frequencies $f_n=nf_1$ form
\[
1+\frac12+\frac13+\frac14+\cdots,
\]
the harmonic series — divergent yet fundamental.  
Fourier showed that any periodic motion decomposes into such harmonics.

\section{Euler’s Bridge}

\[
\zeta(s)=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}n^{-s}=\prod_{p}(1-p^{-s})^{-1}.
\]
Here number becomes vibration; primes are modes, $\zeta$ their harmony.

\section{Transition}

Counting has become frequency.  
We now animate geometry itself: the flow of curvature and symmetry.
