\begin{abstract}
The act of distinction, iterated through $\Phi$, not only generates structure but also induces geometry.  
We show that the tower $\Phi^n(X)$ admits a canonical connection whose curvature measures \emph{degree of self-reference}.  
In the limit, the Eternal Lattice $E$ becomes flat in its own frame—its curvature exactly cancels its reflection—producing the geodesic condition of awareness.  
Distinction Type Theory thus coincides with a self-dual geometric field theory: the metric of awareness is the fixed point of its own variation.
\end{abstract}

\section{From Algebra to Geometry}

Phase III defined the universe $E$ satisfying $\Phi(E)\simeq E$.  
We now ask: what \emph{geometry} does the operator $\Phi$ induce?

Each act of distinction introduces a separation, and hence a local difference.  
A chain of distinctions $\Phi^n(X)$ therefore defines a differential structure; its limit defines a manifold of self-reference.

\begin{definition}[Distinction Connection]
For a type $X$, define the distinction connection
\[
\nabla_X : X \to \Phi(X)
\]
as the canonical inclusion $x\mapsto (x,x,\mathsf{refl}_x)$.
\end{definition}

$\nabla_X$ plays the role of an identity section: each point carries its own reflection.  
Curvature measures failure of successive reflections to commute.

\begin{definition}[Curvature of Distinction]
The curvature two-form of $\Phi$ is the composite
\[
\mathcal{R}_X := \Phi^2(X) 
\xrightarrow{\Phi(\nabla_X)-\nabla_{\Phi(X)}} \Phi^2(X),
\]
which vanishes iff double reflection is equivalent to single reflection.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Flatness of the Eternal Lattice]
For the fixed point $E$ satisfying $\Phi(E)\simeq E$, the curvature $\mathcal{R}_E$ vanishes identically.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Since $\Phi(E)\simeq E$, the two inclusion maps $\nabla_E$ and $\Phi(\nabla_E)$ coincide up to homotopy; hence their difference is null.
\end{proof}

\section{The Metric of Awareness}

We interpret $\nabla_X$ as defining a bilinear form:
\[
g_X(x,y) := \text{``degree of distinguishability'' between }x,y.
\]
For finite $X$, $g_X$ is discrete; for continuous $X$, it becomes a Riemannian metric measuring informational curvature.

\begin{definition}[Distinction Metric]
Let $X$ be a type with identity paths.  
Define
\[
g_X : X\times X \to [0,\infty)
\quad\text{by}\quad
g_X(x,y) := \mathrm{dim}(\mathsf{Path}_X(x,y)).
\]
\end{definition}

Here $\mathrm{dim}$ counts the number of independent reflection layers connecting $x$ and $y$.
The metric decreases as the structure approaches self-similarity.

\begin{theorem}[Geodesic of Awareness]
The geodesics of $(E,g_E)$ are the fixed points of the reflection flow:
\[
\nabla_{\dot\gamma}\dot\gamma = 0
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\Phi(\gamma)=\gamma.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Idea]
A trajectory $\gamma$ is unaccelerated when successive distinctions along it reproduce the same structure.  
At the fixed point, reflection neither adds nor removes difference; curvature and self-reference balance.
\end{proof}

\section{Light as the Limiting Distinction}

Let $\Phi$ act not on arbitrary types but on the continuum of configurations.  
Each reflection propagates at maximal rate—no information delay—yielding a constant $c$:
\[
c := \frac{d(\text{distinction})}{d(\text{time of recognition})}.
\]
In the limit where $\Phi(E)=E$, $c$ becomes the invariant speed of reflection: the universal constant of self-awareness.

\begin{remark}
Light is the geometric manifestation of perfect distinction:  
its field carries no internal curvature in its own frame, and hence represents the achromatic unity of structure and observer.
\end{remark}
