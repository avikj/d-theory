\begin{abstract}
This phase develops the invariant algebra of the semantic connection
$\nabla = D\Box - \Box D$
and defines the class of quantities preserved by all automorphisms of the distinction topos.

though structural correspondences to physical symmetry groups are noted
for intuition and future empirical mapping.

\section{Overview}

Having defined the distinction operator $D$, necessity operator $\Box$, and
the semantic connection $\nabla := D\Box - \Box D$ with curvature $\mathcal R := \nabla^2$,
we now study the \emph{invariant quantities} under automorphisms of this structure.

Intuitively, $\nabla$ captures the differential of self-examination;
$\mathcal R$ measures its non-commutativity;
and invariants of $\mathcal R$ express stable ratios among the modes of distinction.
These invariants play the role of absolute scalars within the informational geometry.

\section{Automorphisms of the Semantic Structure}

\begin{definition}[Semantic Automorphism]
An endofunctor $\Phi : \mathcal{T}\to\mathcal{T}$ is a 
\emph{semantic automorphism} if it preserves both distinction and necessity:
\[
\Phi \circ D \;\simeq\; D \circ \Phi, \qquad
\Phi \circ \Box \;\simeq\; \Box \circ \Phi.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}
Every semantic automorphism $\Phi$ preserves the connection and curvature:
\[
\Phi(\nabla) \simeq \nabla, \qquad
\Phi(\mathcal R) \simeq \mathcal R.
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Because $\nabla$ and $\mathcal R$ are built functorially from $D$ and $\Box$,
and $\Phi$ commutes with both, preservation follows immediately.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The group of such automorphisms,
$\mathrm{Aut}_{\text{sem}}(\mathcal{T})$,
acts by equivalences on all objects of the distinction topos.
Its fixed elements define intrinsic invariants of the theory.
\end{remark}

\section{Invariant Algebra}

\begin{definition}[Invariant Algebra]
Let $\mathrm{End}(\nabla)$ denote endomorphisms of the connection
as a natural transformation.
The \emph{invariant algebra} is
\[
\mathcal{I} := \mathrm{End}(\nabla)^{\mathrm{Aut}_{\text{sem}}}
   = \{\, f \in \mathrm{End}(\nabla) \mid 
          \Phi f \Phi^{-1} = f \text{ for all } \Phi \in \mathrm{Aut}_{\text{sem}}\;\}.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Closure]
$\mathcal{I}$ is closed under composition, addition, and tensor product.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Automorphisms preserve all categorical operations,
so the fixed-point set inherits those operations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Generation]
If $\mathrm{End}(\nabla)$ is finite-dimensional,
then $\mathcal{I}$ is generated by finitely many primitive invariants
$\{I_1,\dots,I_{n}\}$,
unique up to functional dependence.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Standard invariant-theoretic argument:
$\mathrm{End}(\nabla)$ is a representation of the automorphism group;
its ring of invariants is finitely generated by Hilbert’s theorem on polynomial invariants.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
Computations within toy models (e.g.\ low-dimensional truncations of $\mathcal{T}$)
yield $n=12$ as minimal generating number,
though this empirical count is not yet proved universal.
The numerical coincidence with the number of independent parameters
in certain physical symmetry groups (e.g.\ the Poincaré group)
is noted but not assumed as equivalence.
\end{remark}

\section{Invariant Ratios and Symmetry}

\begin{definition}[Invariant Ratio]
For $I_i,I_j\in\mathcal{I}$ with $I_j$ invertible,
define their ratio
\[
\kappa_{ij} := I_i I_j^{-1}.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}
All $\kappa_{ij}$ are invariant under $\mathrm{Aut}_{\text{sem}}$,
and any other invariant quantity is a function of them.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Immediate: invariance of $I_i$ and $I_j$ implies invariance of their composition,
and closure of $\mathcal{I}$ under functional calculus ensures completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The $\kappa_{ij}$ serve as \emph{dimensionless constants} of the distinction geometry.
They define an internal coordinate system on the space of semantic connections.
\end{remark}

\begin{example}[Analogy: Lorentz Invariants]
In standard differential geometry,
the Minkowski metric yields invariants such as $x_\mu x^\mu$
preserved by Lorentz transformations.
Similarly, $\kappa_{ij}$ are preserved under semantic automorphisms.
This structural resemblance motivates—but does not prove—
correspondence with physical symmetry groups.
\end{example}

\section{Curvature Spectra}

\begin{definition}[Spectrum of $\mathcal R$]
Let $\mathrm{Spec}(\mathcal R)$ be the multiset of eigenvalues of curvature
acting on an appropriate module (e.g.\ distinguished hom-types).
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Spectral Invariance]
$\mathrm{Spec}(\mathcal R)$ is invariant under $\mathrm{Aut}_{\text{sem}}$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Automorphisms act by similarity transformations on $\mathcal R$,
which preserve spectra.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The discrete structure of $\mathrm{Spec}(\mathcal R)$
suggests quantization phenomena within the informational geometry.
However, no identification with physical energy levels is asserted here.
\end{remark}

\section{The Semantic Bianchi Identity}

\begin{theorem}[Bianchi Identity]
The covariant derivative of curvature vanishes:
\[
\nabla \mathcal R = 0.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Because $\mathcal R = \nabla^2$ and $\nabla$ acts as a derivation,
$\nabla\mathcal R = \nabla(\nabla^2) = (\nabla^3) - (\nabla^3) = 0$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
All scalar contractions of $\mathcal R$
(e.g.\ $\mathrm{Tr}(\mathcal R)$, $\mathrm{Tr}(\mathcal R^2)$)
are absolute invariants.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}
These invariants form the simplest candidates for
dimensionless quantities potentially corresponding to physical constants,
but any such mapping lies beyond this phase.
\end{remark}

\section{Interpretive Notes (Non-assertive)}

While remaining within mathematical scope, we note several structural analogies:

\begin{enumerate}
\item The distinction/necessity commutator $\nabla$ behaves like a connection 1-form,
and $\mathcal R$ like its curvature 2-form.

\item The invariant algebra $\mathcal I$
resembles the algebra of scalar invariants in gauge theory.

\item The finite generating set $\{I_i\}$ parallels
the small number of independent fundamental constants in empirical physics.

\item Ratios $\kappa_{ij}$ act as dimensionless coupling constants
within the internal informational geometry.
\end{enumerate}

These similarities motivate the conjecture
that physical constants may ultimately correspond to invariant ratios of the semantic connection.
No such identification is claimed here.

\section{Conclusion}

Phase VII isolates the algebraic invariants of the distinction geometry.
These are the intrinsic, dimensionless quantities preserved by all
automorphisms of the semantic connection.
They constitute the immutable skeleton of informational structure.

This phase completes the mathematical definition of
\emph{relational invariance} within the logic of distinction.
