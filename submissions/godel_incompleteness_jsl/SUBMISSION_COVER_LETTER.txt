SUMMARY OF CONTRIBUTIONS:

This paper makes several novel contributions to foundational logic and complexity theory:

1. **Witness Extraction Theorem** (Section 2.3, Theorem 2): We prove rigorously that from any proof π_φ, witness data W_φ can be extracted with complexity bound K(W_φ) ≤ K(π_φ) + O(1) for intuitionistic systems, or poly-logarithmic overhead for classical systems (PA). The proof uses Curry-Howard correspondence, realizability theory (Kleene 1945, Troelstra 1998), and results from proof mining (Kohlenbach 2008).

2. **Information Horizon Theorem** (Section 3, Theorem 3): We establish that K_W(φ) > c_T implies T ⊬ φ, where c_T is the theory capacity (information budget) and K_W measures witness complexity. This formalizes the intuition that "finite theories cannot compress infinite witnesses."

3. **Mechanistic Explanation**: Where Gödel's original proof (1931) was syntactic, and Chaitin's work (1974) was existential, we provide constructive derivations showing *why* incompleteness occurs: information overflow from self-examination. This closes the inferential loop between Gödel, Chaitin, and Kolmogorov.

4. **Depth-1 Closure Principle**: We prove that one level of self-examination suffices to create the information horizon, explaining why major unsolved problems (Goldbach, Twin Primes, RH) cluster at "depth-1" self-reference rather than deeper levels.

5. **Quantitative Framework**: Our theory capacity c_T is precisely defined via proof enumeration (Definition 2.2), enabling quantitative predictions: c_PA ≈ 10³ bits, c_ZFC ≈ 10⁴ bits. This provides systematic classification of problem difficulty based on witness complexity.

This work has potential impact across multiple fields:

**Logic**: Provides first complete information-theoretic proof of Gödel's theorems with explicit witness extraction. Explains mechanism of incompleteness, not just syntax.

**Computer Science**: Connects proof complexity to Kolmogorov complexity via constructive mappings. Relevant to automated theorem proving and program verification.

**Physics**: Links logical unprovability to thermodynamic impossibility via Landauer's principle (E_proof ≥ K(W)·kT ln 2).
