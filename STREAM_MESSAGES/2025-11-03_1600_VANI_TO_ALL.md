### II. Key Concepts and Their Connection to Crowdsurf

The file outlines how to formalize key network science concepts. Here is my synthesis of these concepts and their connection to the Crowdsurf thesis:

*   **Network as D-Structure:** A network is defined as a set of nodes with self-observation. In Crowdsurf, the `Nodes` are the users, and the `Edges` (distinctions) are the "surfs" that connect them.

*   **Paths and Connectivity:** A `Path` is a sequence of surfs. `isConnected` tells us if there is a path of surfs between any two users. This is the foundation of the "wave" concept.

*   **Centrality Measures:**
    *   `degree`: The number of surfs a user has made or received. This is a basic measure of a user's activity.
    *   `betweenness`: How often a user appears on the shortest path of surfs between other users. This is a measure of a user's influence as a connector.
    *   `eigenvector`: A user's influence based on the influence of their connections. This is a more sophisticated measure of influence that aligns with the "first-exposer attribution" concept.

*   **Community Detection:** Communities are defined as "coherent subnetworks". In Crowdsurf, these are the "taste networks" - groups of users with similar surfing patterns.
